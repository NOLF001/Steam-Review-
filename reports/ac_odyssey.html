⚠ Ubisoft Connect DRM Warning
As of 2024–2026, the dominant negative in reviews is not gameplay — it's Ubisoft Connect. The launcher requires internet for a single-player game, has corrupted player save files, and cannot be bypassed if it goes offline. After The Crew's delisting, players cite EULA language allowing Ubisoft to revoke game licenses. Many negative reviewers describe the game itself positively but mark "Not Recommended" solely due to UConnect dependency. Buy on sale. Back up saves manually.

✓ What Players Love

  • Ancient Greece setting is a genuine work of art
    Every corner of the map — from the Aegean islands to the streets of Athens — is painstakingly reconstructed with historical and mythological detail. Multiple players report booking real flights to Greece after playing. Historians have cited the game's Athens reconstruction as a legitimate visual reference. The sunrise over the Aegean is, simply, one of the most beautiful scenes in gaming.
  • Kassandra is one of the best protagonists in the franchise's history
    The canonical choice (confirmed lore) and widely preferred for voice performance, Kassandra delivers lines with genuine wit, aggression, and emotional range. Her character is described as 'passion and determination made playable.' Players with 1,000+ hours cite her as their all-time favourite game character. Play as her.
  • Sheer content volume rewards long-term players
    1,387h players are still finding quests they hadn't seen. The Fate of Atlantis DLC alone is described as 'longer and more enjoyable than many full games.' Main story + both expansions + side content fills 140-200 hours. The map is so dense that even completionists are surprised after multiple playthroughs.
  • Greek mythology integration done right
    You fight the Minotaur, the Medusa, the Cyclops, and entire armies of Spartans. Socrates, Hippocrates, and Herodotus are in-game characters with actual philosophical dialogue. The Fate of Atlantis expansion is fan-favourite for transforming into something closer to myth-fantasy than action game. 'The Witcher 3 but in Ancient Greece' is the community's go-to description.
  • Combat flexibility and build variety
    Three distinct playstyles (Warrior, Assassin, Hunter) each have deep ability trees. The Spartan Kick becomes iconic. Naval combat with sea shanties is specifically loved. Late-game build variety enables demigod power fantasies: poison builds, fire combos, legendary weapons. Nightmare mode offers genuine challenge for veteran players.
  • Fate of Atlantis DLC is a genuine masterpiece
    Consistently cited separately from the base game as a quality peak. Three mythological realms (Elysium, Underworld, Atlantis) each with distinct mechanics. The canon AC lore revelations in Atlantis are among the most significant in the series. Multiple reviewers who were lukewarm on the base game rate the DLC a 10/10.

✕ Common Criticisms

  • Ubisoft Connect DRM is the game's biggest enemy — not its design
    The #1 negative driver in 2024-2026 reviews is not gameplay but Ubisoft Connect. The launcher: requires internet for a single-player game, has corrupted save files (one 100h player lost all progress), goes offline unpredictably, and — after The Crew was delisted — players cite EULA clauses allowing Ubisoft to revoke licenses at any time. Dozens of reviewers rate the game positively but mark Not Recommended solely due to UConnect.
  • Enemy level scaling eliminates power progression
    Enemies scale with the player's level throughout the entire game. The community consensus: 'You never feel stronger, no matter how much you grind.' Returning to areas you've outleveled to dominate them — a staple satisfaction of RPGs — is gone. Every encounter remains artificially difficult. Paired with XP-booster microtransactions in a single-player game, this is the most criticized pure game-design decision.
  • Repetitive open-world content — 'wide as an ocean, deep as a puddle'
    The map is full of identical bandit camps, conquest battles, and fetch quests. 'Go here, kill this guy, come back' applies to 80% of side content. The main story requires XP-grinding side quests to unlock story missions. Copy-paste camp design is acknowledged by even enthusiastic recommenders. After 80+ hours the formula wears thin for most players.
  • Not a traditional Assassin's Creed game
    Stealth is optional and often impractical — enemies don't die to a single stab. The hidden blade is absent until DLC. Parkour is simplified. The franchise's signature tools are deprioritized in favor of RPG combat. Long-time AC fans who wanted a successor to the Ezio trilogy will be disappointed. New players coming for an RPG experience will be pleasantly surprised.
  • Microtransactions in a single-player game
    XP boosters are sold in the in-game store, while the base game's XP curve feels tuned to push purchases. The intersection of level scaling + XP grind + paid boosters is the most cynical part of Ubisoft's monetization strategy. Mods exist to adjust the XP multiplier and are widely recommended.

Verdict

Assassin's Creed Odyssey is the franchise at its most ambitious and most bloated simultaneously. The Ancient Greece setting is a genuine triumph — a living world of such beauty and historical authenticity that players book real flights to Athens afterward. Kassandra is one of the best protagonist performances Ubisoft has ever produced. The Fate of Atlantis DLC stands among the best expansion content in gaming. By almost every measure of craft and ambition, this is a remarkable achievement.

The asterisks are real, though. Enemy level scaling destroys the power fantasy. The XP grind feels tuned to sell boosters. Identical bandit camps fill the map for 150 hours. And in 2024-2026, the dominant negative force is no longer a design choice but a corporate one: Ubisoft Connect. A DRM launcher that requires internet for offline single-player, has corrupted save files, and carries EULA language allowing revocation of access. After The Crew's delisting, the community's anxiety about "not owning" their $60 game is understandable and documented.

Buy it on sale — 50%+ off, which Ubisoft offers frequently. Play as Kassandra. Do the Fate of Atlantis DLC. Accept that you are playing a Greek mythology open-world RPG, not an Assassin's Creed game. Stop before the map icon fatigue sets in. That version of this game is a 9/10. The version where you play it full price, expect stealthy assassination gameplay, and lose your 150h save to a UConnect outage is a 3/10. Know which one you're buying.
