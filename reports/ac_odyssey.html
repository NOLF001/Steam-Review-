Pros
  • ►Ancient Greece setting is a genuine work of art — every corner meticulously reconstructed.
    Multiple players report booking real flights to Greece after playing; historians cite the Athens reconstruction as a visual reference.
  • ►Kassandra is one of the best protagonist performances in the franchise's history.
    Wit, aggression, and emotional range; players with 1,000+ hours name her their all-time favourite character. Play as her.
  • ►Sheer content volume rewards long-term players — 1,387h players still finding new quests.
    Main story + both DLCs + side content fills 140–200 hours; Fate of Atlantis DLC alone beats many full games in length.
  • ►Greek mythology integration done right — fight the Minotaur, Medusa, and Cyclops.
    Socrates and Herodotus appear as in-game characters; 'The Witcher 3 but in Ancient Greece' is the community shorthand.
  • ►Three distinct playstyles (Warrior, Assassin, Hunter) with deep ability trees and late-game power fantasies.
    Spartan Kick is iconic; naval combat with sea shanties is specifically loved across the corpus.
  • ►Fate of Atlantis DLC is a standout masterpiece — three mythological realms with distinct mechanics.
    Multiple reviewers who were lukewarm on the base game rate the DLC a 10/10.
Cons
  • ►Ubisoft Connect DRM is the dominant negative driver — not gameplay.
    Requires internet for single-player, has corrupted saves (one 100h player lost everything), and carries EULA language allowing license revocation.
  • ►Enemy level scaling eliminates power progression — you never feel stronger, no matter how much you grind.
    Paired with XP-booster microtransactions in a single-player game, this is the most criticized design decision.
  • ►Repetitive open-world content — identical bandit camps fill the map for 150+ hours.
    'Wide as an ocean, deep as a puddle'; 80% of side quests are 'go here, kill this guy, come back.'
  • ►Not a traditional AC game — stealth is optional, hidden blade absent until DLC, parkour simplified.
    Long-time fans expecting Ezio-style gameplay will be disappointed; RPG newcomers will be pleasantly surprised.
  • ►XP boosters sold while the XP curve feels tuned to push purchases.
    Level scaling + XP grind + paid boosters is Ubisoft's most cynical single-player monetization combo; mods widely recommended.
Analyst Verdict

Assassin's Creed Odyssey is the franchise at its most ambitious and most bloated simultaneously. The Ancient Greece setting is a genuine triumph — a living world of such beauty and historical authenticity that players book real flights to Athens afterward. Kassandra is one of the best protagonist performances Ubisoft has ever produced. The Fate of Atlantis DLC stands among the best expansion content in gaming. By almost every measure of craft and ambition, this is a remarkable achievement.

The asterisks are real, though. Enemy level scaling destroys the power fantasy. The XP grind feels tuned to sell boosters. Identical bandit camps fill the map for 150 hours. And in 2024–2026, the dominant negative force is no longer a design choice but a corporate one: Ubisoft Connect. A DRM launcher that requires internet for offline single-player, has corrupted save files, and carries EULA language allowing revocation of access. After The Crew's delisting, the community's anxiety about 'not owning' their $60 game is understandable and documented.

Buy it on sale — 50%+ off, which Ubisoft offers frequently. Play as Kassandra. Do the Fate of Atlantis DLC. Accept that you are playing a Greek mythology open-world RPG, not an Assassin’s Creed game. Stop before the map icon fatigue sets in. That version of this game is a 9/10. The version where you play it full price, expect stealthy assassination gameplay, and lose your 150h save to a UConnect outage is a 3/10. Know which one you’re buying.
